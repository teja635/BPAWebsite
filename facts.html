Types of Drugs

Narcotics

Narcotics are opioids and are originally referred to substances that can relieve or dull pain. A common misconception of narcotics is that all drugs are narcotics. This statement is not true and some actual narcotics are listed below to clarify this misconception. There are two types of narcotics which are man made or occur naturally. Those that come from the natural world comes from poppy papaver somniferum, which is the source of all natural opioids. Those that are man made come from a mixture of chemicals and other harmful substances and form destructive substances such as methadone, fentanyl, and meperidine.
The effect of narcotics on the body affects the mind and body itself. Its psychological effects is physiological dependence which means the mind will depend on the use of these drugs as a coping mechanism when the environment is too stressful for the individual. Its effects on the body include but are not limited to constriction of the pupils, flushing of the face and neck, nausea, vomiting, and slowed breathing. The body will become dependent on the drugs and withdrawal will become physically harder if drug use is discontinued. Some of the severe withdrawal symptoms are severe depression, chills alternating with flushing, and excessive sweating, and restlessness.
There are five classes of narcotics ranging from Schedule I to Schedule V narcotics. Schedule I narcotics have no medical use and are illegal to sell, purchase, and use in the United States. If caught doing any of these activities with the drug then it will result in a fine up to five million dollars and imprisonment from five to forty years on the first offense. Two or more offenses will result in a fine up to ten million dollars and anywhere between ten years to life in imprisonment.

  • Heroin
  • Hydromorphone
  • Methadone

Central Nervous System Stimulants

Stimulants are drugs that speed up the body’s systems. And come in all shapes and sizes in the forms of capsules and powder. When large doses are consumed, users reach a high and only stop use when they have either ran out or have reached a state of psychosis and physical exhaustion. When under the influence of stimulants, users try their best to recreate that same rush that they have felt when the drug first entered their system.
The effect of stimulants on the mind is to create a sense of exhilaration, but have negative side effects as well. These include aggression, suicidal and homicidal tendencies, and hallucinations may occur while under the influence. These drugs cause the mind to become more dependent on the drug as a coping mechanism. The effect of the drug on the body is that it is supposed to reverse the effect of fatigue on both mental and physical tasks. But these drugs can cause abdominal cramps, tremors, excessive vomiting, and chest pain and palpitations. Overdose of the drug may result in convulsions and cardiovascular collapse resulting in death. Stimulants have no medical use and are therefore illegal to purchase, sell, and use in the United States. If caught doing any of these activities then the punishment will be the same as the punishment with purchasing, selling, and using narcotics.

  • Amphetamines
  • Cocaine
  • Khat
  • Methamphetamine

Central Nervous System Depressants

Depressants aid an individual to sleep, relieve anxiety and muscle spasms, and prevent seizures. People can quickly become dependent on this drug which means that one would have to take increased dosages in order to feel and function normally. This leads to an increased chance of coma and death. This drug is used to achieve a high and is combined with other drugs to get a desired effect. Abusers usually take higher dosages than individuals who are taking the drug under supervision of a medical professional. The effect of depressants on the mind is amnesia while under the influence of a depressant, reduced reaction time, impaired mental function and can cause confusion. The effect on the body is that the drugs can cause slurred speech, slowed breathing, lowered blood pressure, vomiting and nausea, and blurred vision. The abuse of depressants can cause dependence and tolerance making it almost impossible to overcome the addiction. An overdose of these drugs can cause a coma and can be fatal.
These drugs are legal in the United States only for medical use. The abuse of depressants have the same consequences as the central nervous system stimulants.

  • Valium®
  • Xanax®
  • Halcion®
  • Ativan®
  • Klonopin®
  • Restoril®

Hallucinogens

Hallucinogens can be found in many plants and fungi or is synthetically produced by chemicals. These drugs known to alter human perception and mood. These drugs are made in a variety of forms but are mainly colorful and vibrant to attract young abusers. LSD, ecstasy, and MDMA are among the most popular hallucinogens among high school juniors and seniors. The effect of hallucinogens on the mind is that it can cause many perceptual distortions while under the influence. Some of the more noticeable effects is colors seem to change, flashbacks may occur if the individual is stressed, and tie seems to either slow down or stand still. The physical effects of these drugs is an elevated heart rate, increased blood pressure, and dilated pupils. An overdose of hallucinogens can cause coma, respiratory depression, and death due to respiratory arrest.
Hallucinogens have no medical use in the United States and are therefore illegal. The punishment if caught with these drugs can be imprisonment and fines up to ten million dollars.

  • Lysergic acid diethylamide (LSD)
  • MDMA
  • ecstasy

Inhalants

Inhaling is the process of inhaling dangerous gases and vapors. The source of these can be everyday items and are inexpensive so it attracts many younger teenagers. By the time students reach the eighth grade one in five students have used an inhalant. Hispanic and American Indian students show high rates of usage of inhalants. The effect that these drugs can have on an individual is loss of consciousness, damage to the heart, bone marrow, brain liver and other organs. These drugs can also cause the mind and body to become dependent on its use which can make the withdrawal process a real challenge to these young individuals. An overdose of these poisonous vapors can be fatal and many cases have reported of deaths because of inhalants. Inhalants are everyday household items and are therefore not illegal here in the United States. If a teenager is caught using inhalants they are usually referred to a rehabilitation center to overcome the addiction.

  • Paint Thinners and Removers
  • Gasoline
  • Felt Tip Marker Fluids
  • Hairspray

Prescription

Prescription drugs are any drugs that a doctor prescribes to the patient. These drugs are only abused when an individual takes a dosage that’s higher than the prescribed amount. They are easy to get a hold of and many more Americans are abusing prescription drugs more and more everyday.
The effect of the abuse of prescription drugs depends on the drugs that are being abused. They usually cause a physiological dependence and can ruin the liver and other organs of the body. The body and mind will become dependent on these drugs as the usage of them increases. An overdose of prescription drugs can be fatal to the abuser. These drugs are legal in the United State and if caught abusing them, the individual is usually sent to a rehabilitation center to help overcome the addiction.

  • Hydrocodone
  • Lisinopril
  • Ibuprofen
